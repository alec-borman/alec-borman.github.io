Case Study & Reference Compiler

Tenuto Reference Compiler (tenutoc)

The Scope & Problem: Digital music representation is broken. Formats like MusicXML are deeply visual—obsessed with where ink sits on a page—making them incredibly verbose, fragile to edit, and hostile to version control. I designed Tenuto, a declarative Domain-Specific Language (DSL) that bridges this semantic gap by strictly separating instrument physics from musical logic.

The Architecture: I engineered tenutoc, the official Rust-based compiler pipeline. It utilizes a deterministic LL(1) parser (`chumsky`), robust lexical analysis (`logos`), and a Rational Temporal Engine to guarantee mathematically perfect timing devoid of floating-point drift. .

AI as a Force Multiplier: Building a strict language parser from scratch is a massive undertaking. I leveraged LLMs heavily, but not for "vibe coding." I operated as the primary Architect, dictating strict parsing rules, managing memory states, and enforcing test coverage. Tenuto is uniquely optimized for AI generation, reducing MusicXML's ~150 tokens down to 7 for a standard measure, natively solving context-window limitations for AI composers.
